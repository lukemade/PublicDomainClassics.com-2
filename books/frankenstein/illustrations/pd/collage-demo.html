Letter 1 · Walton to Margaret

I try in vain to be persuaded that the pole is the seat of frost and desolation; it ever presents itself to my imagination as the region of beauty and delight.

Letter 1 · Walton to Margaret

There, Margaret, the sun is for ever visible, its broad disk just skirting the horizon and diffusing a perpetual splendour. What may not be expected in a country of eternal light?

Letter 1 · Walton to Margaret

I have read with ardour the accounts of the various voyages which have been made in the prospect of arriving at the North Pacific Ocean through the seas which surround the pole. These volumes were my study day and night.

J. H. Colton, Map of the Arctic or North Pole, 1855

Letter 1 · Walton to Margaret

I accompanied the whale-fishers on several expeditions to the North Sea; I voluntarily endured cold, famine, thirst, and want of sleep; I often worked harder than the common sailors during the day.

Abraham Storck, Dutch Whalers near Spitsbergen, 1690  ·  North Cape, Norway, photochrome, c. 1890s  ·  William Bradford, An Incident of Whaling, c. 1870

Letter 1 · Walton to Margaret

Twice I actually hired myself as an under-mate in a Greenland whaler, and acquitted myself to admiration. I must own I felt a little proud when my captain offered me the second dignity in the vessel.

Frank Hurley, Endurance trapped in pack ice, 1915  ·  The Fram in Arctic ice, 1896  ·  William Bradford, American Whaler in Arctic Waters, c. 1880

Letter 1 · Walton to Margaret

This is the most favourable period for travelling in Russia. They fly quickly over the snow in their sledges; the motion is pleasant. I have no ambition to lose my life on the post-road between St. Petersburgh and Archangel.

Nikolai Sverchkov, A Troika Sleigh in Russia, c. 1850s
